Bruce Springsteen

Bruce Frederick Joseph Springsteen (born September 23, 1949) is an American singer-songwriter. He is known for his work with the E Street Band. Springsteen is widely known for his brand of poetic lyrics, Americana, working class and sometimes political sentiments centered on his native New Jersey, his distinctive voice, and lengthy, energetic stage performances. He has sold more 120 million records worldwide and more than 64 million records in the United States, making him one of the world's best-selling artists of all time. He has earned numerous awards and honors, including 20 Grammy Awards and an Academy Award. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1999 and the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 2004. In 2009, Springsteen was named the MusiCares person of the year and in 2013 was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the nation's highest civilian honor, by President Barack Obama. He married Patti Scialfa in 1991, and the couple have two daughters.

Albums

Born to Run

Born to Run is the sixth studio album by American singer-songwriter Bruce Springsteen. It was released on January 25, 1975, by Columbia Records. Produced by Springsteen and Jon Landau, it is widely regarded as Springsteen's breakthrough record and one of the most important albums in rock and roll history. The album features the hit singles "Thunder Road", "Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out", "Night" and "Backstreets".

Born in the U.S.A.

Born in the U.S.A. is the seventh studio album by American singer-songwriter Bruce Springsteen. It was released on June 4, 1984, by Columbia Records. The album was Springsteen's first release on the label, and was produced by Jon Landau and Springsteen. It was recorded at The Power Station in New York City and at The Record Plant in Los Angeles. The album was Springsteen's first to reach number one on the Billboard 200, and was certified 12× Platinum by the RIAA. It was also his first album to top the charts in the United Kingdom, where it was certified 6× Platinum by the BPI. The album was nominated for Album of the Year at the 27th Grammy Awards, but lost to Michael Jackson's Thriller.
